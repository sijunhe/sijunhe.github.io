Looks like somebody has stumbled upon my blog!

My name is Sijun He. I am a data scientist at [Autodesk](http://www.autodesk.com/), where I mostly do analytics & machine learning on products(i.e. [AutoCAD](https://www.autodesk.com/products/autocad/overview), [Revit](https://www.autodesk.com/products/revit-family/overview), [Maya](https://www.autodesk.com/products/maya/overview), etc.) that help engineers designing [towering skyscrapers](https://www.autodesk.com/solutions/bim/hub/meet-shanghai-tower-chinas-tallest-building) and filemakers crafting [Oscar-winning visual effects](http://usa.autodesk.com/adsk/servlet/item%3FsiteID%3D123112%26id%3D15300670).

In the summer of 2015, I graduated from [Rose-Hulman Institiute of Technology](http://www.rose-hulman.edu/) and was heading to Stanford for my graduate study in [Structural Engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Structural_engineering). Accidentally, I found my passion in data science and never looked back. 

The blog documents my journey of learning data science from scratch. It will mostly be reading notes and personal projects I have done for work and school. The blog is a journal for myself. Whenever I am upset about not making enough progress (happens ALL THE TIME), I read my blog and remind myself of how far I have gone. If you like the blog, I encourage you to have one for yourself.
